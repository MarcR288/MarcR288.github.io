Hi there!

I am from Winnipeg, MB. I joined the Canadian Armed Forces (CAF) Primary Reserves in the summer of 2013, just before my senior year of high school. I joined to gain life experience and earn enough money to fund my post-secondary education, where I studied Criminal Justice at the University of Winnipeg.
Halfway through my studies at the University of Winnipeg, I transferred to the University of Manitoba to study Criminology and gain extra credits for military training. After graduating with a B.A. in Criminology from the University of Manitoba, I was selected for a full-time position with the Transition Group in the CAF, where I worked until 2022, when I was posted to Canadian Forces Base Halifax.
I chose to study IT Programming at the Nova Scotia Community College after realizing that a long-term career in the CAF was no longer the right fit for me. I've always had an interest in computer science and even considered it as my major in university. After moving to Nova Scotia, I was much closer to being able to start a family and wanted to control where I lived.
Looking ahead, I see myself working in software development, focusing on creating solutions that tackle real-world problems. I consider myself highly motivated and very excited to contribute to building technology that makes a meaningful impact.
